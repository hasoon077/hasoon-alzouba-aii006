Conclusions & Analysis

Online Shopping is the Norm

85% of our survey participants have made at least one online purchase — reflecting the broader global trend. According to eMarketer, global e-commerce sales have grown consistently year over year, driven largely by convenience, competitive pricing, and expanded mobile access. Our survey aligns with Pew Internet data showing that the vast majority of internet users in developed and developing countries engage in online retail.

Younger Users Lead Adoption

50% of respondents fell in the 18–25 age bracket, and this group also showed the highest purchase frequency. This is consistent with findings from ClickZ and eMarketer, which report that Millennials and Gen Z are the most digitally native shoppers, preferring mobile commerce and apps over traditional in-store experiences. The pattern reinforces the need for brands to invest in mobile-first e-commerce strategies.

Education Correlates with Online Buying Confidence

Respondents with college-level education or higher made up 70% of active online buyers. Higher education levels are associated with greater digital literacy and trust in online payment systems. According to Pew Internet research, educated users are more likely to feel confident entering financial information online and navigating return/refund processes — key barriers for non-buyers.

Monthly Frequency Dominates

45% of buyers shop online once per month. This moderate but consistent frequency suggests that e-commerce has become a habitual part of consumer behavior rather than an occasional novelty. E-Commerce Times reports that subscription services and loyalty programs are key drivers encouraging repeat monthly purchases on platforms like Amazon Prime and local equivalents.
